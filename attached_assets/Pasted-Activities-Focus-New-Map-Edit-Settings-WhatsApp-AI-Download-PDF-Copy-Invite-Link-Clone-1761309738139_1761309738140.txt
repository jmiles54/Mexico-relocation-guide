Explore Historic Downtown Puerto Vallarta on Foot
Begin your journey with a guided walking tour through the charming cobblestone streets of Old Town, discovering iconic landmarks like the Church of Our Lady of Guadalupe and vibrant local markets.

Explore Historic Downtown Puerto Vallarta on Foot is a fantastic way to experience the vibrant culture, architecture, and local life.

Tips:

Wear comfortable walking shoes as the cobblestone streets can be uneven.
Bring a hat and sunscreen since it can get quite sunny.
Start early in the morning or late afternoon to avoid the midday heat.
Visit landmarks such as the Church of Our Lady of Guadalupe, the Malecon boardwalk, and the Olas Altas area.
Stop by local markets and shops for souvenirs and artisan crafts.
Enjoy local eateries and try Mexican street food along the way.
Stay hydrated by carrying a bottle of water.
Guided walking tours are available and often include historical insights.
Price:

Walking around is generally free unless you opt for a guided tour.
Guided tour prices can range from $15 to $40 USD depending on the length and inclusions.
Other:

Downtown Puerto Vallarta is very safe for tourists during the day.
Keep some cash handy for small purchases.
Best visited during the dry season (November to May) for pleasant weather.
